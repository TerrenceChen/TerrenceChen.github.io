Bio
======

I am a political and historical sociologist studying patterns of political contestation and public discourse in democratic states. My work combines computational text analysis and qualitative archival methods. 

I am currently a PhD Candidate in Sociology at New York University. I received my B.A. in Sociology from National Taiwan University (2021).


Research
======

My dissertation, "Fiscalization of Politics: Budget Talk as Political Justification in the U.S., 1921-2024" focuses on how U.S. politicians use "budget talk," namely discourse around budget deficits, government debt, and saving taxpayer money, to discuss, challenge, and justify public policies in the last hundred years. I seek to understand why Democrats have become the champion of fiscal responsibility in the recent fifty years while Republicans have become "fiscal opportunists" who primarily use budget talk when they are out of power.

My previous work on Taiwan's digital democracy focuses on how Taiwan as a young democracy started to promote digital tools to promote democratic participation, as well as the conditions under which these new tools allow citizens to influence policy changes. My work has been published in [Third World Quaterly](https://doi.org/10.1080/01436597.2022.2042801), [Big Data & Society](https://doi.org/10.1177/20539517241296038), and [Information, Communication & Society](https://doi.org/10.1080/1369118X.2025.2558150).
